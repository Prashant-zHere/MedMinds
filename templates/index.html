Understand Common Medical Terms

Hb / HGB

Hemoglobin

The protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen throughout your body. Low levels indicate anemia.

Normal: 12-16 g/dL (Women)
13-17 g/dL (Men)
WBC

White Blood Cells

Fight infections and diseases. High levels may indicate infection, low levels may suggest immune problems.

Normal: 4,500-11,000 cells/μL
Platelets

Thrombocytes

Help in blood clotting. Abnormal levels can cause bleeding disorders or clotting issues.

Normal: 150,000-450,000/μL
RBC

Red Blood Cells

Carry oxygen to tissues. Low count indicates anemia, high count suggests polycythemia.

Normal: 4.2-5.4 million/μL (Women)
4.7-6.1 million/μL (Men)
HCT

Hematocrit

Percentage of blood volume made up by red blood cells. Measures blood thickness.

Normal: 36-46% (Women)
42-52% (Men)
MCV

Mean Corpuscular Volume

Average size of red blood cells. Helps diagnose type of anemia (microcytic or macrocytic).

Normal: 80-100 fL
ALT

Alanine Aminotransferase

Liver enzyme released when liver is damaged. High levels indicate liver inflammation or damage.

Normal: 7-56 U/L
AST

Aspartate Aminotransferase

Enzyme found in liver, heart, and muscles. High levels suggest liver or muscle damage.

Normal: 10-40 U/L
ALP

Alkaline Phosphatase

Enzyme related to bile ducts. Elevated in bile duct obstruction or bone disorders.

Normal: 44-147 U/L
Bilirubin

Total Bilirubin

Breakdown product of red blood cells. High levels cause jaundice (yellow skin/eyes).

Normal: 0.1-1.2 mg/dL
Albumin

Serum Albumin

Main protein made by liver. Low levels indicate liver disease or malnutrition.

Normal: 3.5-5.0 g/dL
GGT

Gamma-Glutamyl Transferase

Liver enzyme sensitive to alcohol. High levels suggest alcohol abuse or bile duct problems.

Normal: 9-48 U/L (Women)
12-58 U/L (Men)
TSH

Thyroid Stimulating Hormone

Controls thyroid hormone production. High TSH = underactive thyroid, Low TSH = overactive thyroid.

Normal: 0.4-4.0 mIU/L
T4

Thyroxine

Main thyroid hormone. Regulates metabolism, growth, and development.

Normal: 5-12 μg/dL
T3

Triiodothyronine

Active thyroid hormone. More potent than T4 in affecting body metabolism.

Normal: 100-200 ng/dL
Free T4

Free Thyroxine

Active form of T4 not bound to proteins. More accurate measure of thyroid function.

Normal: 0.8-1.8 ng/dL
Anti-TPO

Thyroid Peroxidase Antibodies

Antibodies against thyroid. High levels indicate autoimmune thyroid disease (Hashimoto's).

Positive: >9 IU/mL (Autoimmune)
Anti-Tg

Thyroglobulin Antibodies

Antibodies against thyroglobulin. Elevated in autoimmune thyroid disorders.

Positive: >4 IU/mL (Autoimmune)
Creatinine

Serum Creatinine

Waste product from muscle metabolism. High levels indicate poor kidney function.

Normal: 0.6-1.2 mg/dL (Men)
0.5-1.1 mg/dL (Women)
eGFR

Estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate

Measures how well kidneys filter waste. Best indicator of kidney function.

Normal: >90 mL/min
Kidney Disease: <60 mL/min
BUN

Blood Urea Nitrogen

Measures urea nitrogen in blood. High levels may indicate kidney problems or dehydration.

Normal: 7-20 mg/dL
Uric Acid

Serum Uric Acid

Waste product from purine breakdown. High levels can cause gout or kidney stones.

Normal: 3.4-7.0 mg/dL (Men)
2.4-6.0 mg/dL (Women)
Albumin/Creatinine Ratio

Urine ACR

Measures albumin in urine. Early indicator of kidney damage, especially in diabetics.

Normal: <30 mg/g
Kidney Damage: >30 mg/g
Cystatin C

Serum Cystatin C

Alternative marker for kidney function. Less affected by muscle mass than creatinine.

Normal: 0.53-0.95 mg/L
HbA1c

Glycated Hemoglobin

Average blood sugar levels over past 2-3 months. Used to diagnose and monitor diabetes.

Normal: Below 5.7%
Pre-diabetes: 5.7-6.4%
Diabetes: Above 6.5%
FBS

Fasting Blood Sugar

Blood glucose after 8+ hours of fasting. Screens for diabetes and pre-diabetes.

Normal: 70-99 mg/dL
Pre-diabetes: 100-125 mg/dL
Diabetes: >126 mg/dL
PPBS

Postprandial Blood Sugar

Blood glucose 2 hours after eating. Measures body's ability to handle glucose.

Normal: <140 mg/dL
Pre-diabetes: 140-199 mg/dL
Diabetes: >200 mg/dL
OGTT

Oral Glucose Tolerance Test

Measures body's response to sugar. Gold standard for diabetes diagnosis.

Normal: <140 mg/dL (2hr)
Diabetes: >200 mg/dL (2hr)
Insulin

Fasting Insulin

Hormone that regulates blood sugar. High levels indicate insulin resistance.

Normal: 2.6-24.9 μIU/mL
C-Peptide

Connecting Peptide

Byproduct of insulin production. Measures how much insulin body is making.

Normal: 0.5-2.0 ng/mL
